SALSA

Speech Analysis for Low-compute Systems with Accessibility

speech-analysis low-compute accessible

Accessible Diagnosis Through Speech

Speech patterns can reveal early signs of cognitive decline, offering a non-invasive and accessible method for Alzheimer's detection. However, most existing systems require expensive computational resources or specialized equipment, limiting their deployment in everyday clinical settings or at-home monitoring scenarios.

A Lightweight Approach

SALSA (Speech Analysis for Low-compute Systems with Accessibility) is specifically designed to overcome these barriers. It's a lightweight framework that can detect Alzheimer's disease from spontaneous speech while running efficiently on consumer-grade hardware—making it practical for widespread deployment.

Technical Architecture

The framework employs an innovative approach using frozen pre-trained encoders combined with LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) adapters. This design choice offers several advantages:

  • Efficiency: By keeping the base encoders frozen, we dramatically reduce computational requirements during both training and inference.
  • Multimodal analysis: SALSA combines acoustic embeddings with lexical cues, capturing both how someone speaks and what they say.
  • Generalization: The architecture maintains strong performance across different datasets and recording conditions.

Strong Cross-Dataset Performance

One of the critical challenges in medical AI is ensuring models work well beyond their training data. SALSA demonstrates strong cross-corpus performance on established benchmarks like ADReSS 2020, meaning it can generalize to new speakers, recording environments, and speech tasks without significant performance degradation.

This robustness is crucial for real-world deployment, where conditions may vary significantly from controlled research settings.

Acoustic and Lexical Features

SALSA's dual approach captures complementary aspects of speech:

  • Acoustic features: Voice quality, rhythm, pauses, and prosody that may indicate cognitive processing difficulties.
  • Lexical features: Word choice, semantic content, and linguistic patterns that can reveal memory or language difficulties.

By combining these modalities, SALSA achieves more reliable detection than systems relying on either aspect alone.

Practical Deployment

The framework's low computational requirements mean it can run on standard laptops, tablets, or even smartphones. This accessibility opens up possibilities for:

  • Regular at-home monitoring for early detection
  • Deployment in primary care settings without specialized equipment
  • Use in underserved communities with limited access to advanced diagnostic tools
  • Scalable screening programs for at-risk populations

Privacy-Conscious Design

Since SALSA can run locally on consumer hardware, it enables privacy-preserving deployment where sensitive speech data never needs to leave the user's device—an important consideration for healthcare applications.
