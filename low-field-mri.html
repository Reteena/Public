Medical Imaging & Deep Learning Project 02

Low Field MRI Framework (Clarity)

deep-learning image-segmentation open-source

The Challenge

Traditional high-field MRI machines are expensive, require specialized facilities, and can be inaccessible to many communities, particularly in underserved areas. Low-field MRI offers a more affordable and accessible alternative, but the image quality has traditionally been insufficient for precise diagnostic purposes, particularly for conditions like Alzheimer's Disease that require detailed brain imaging.

Our Solution

Clarity is a deep learning framework that bridges this gap by enhancing low-field MRI scans to enable accurate segmentation of brain regions linked to Alzheimer's Disease. By leveraging advanced neural networks, we can extract clinically valuable information from lower-quality scans that would otherwise be difficult to interpret.

Performance

The framework achieved an impressive 96% accuracy in automatic Alzheimer's Disease diagnosis using volumetric data combined with ensemble machine learning models. This level of accuracy rivals that of traditional high-field MRI analysis, demonstrating that cost-effective alternatives can maintain diagnostic precision.

Technical Approach

Clarity employs state-of-the-art deep learning architectures specifically optimized for medical image segmentation. The system is trained to identify and segment key brain structures that are particularly affected by Alzheimer's Disease, including the hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, and other regions known to show early atrophy.

Our ensemble approach combines multiple models to ensure robust and reliable predictions, reducing the risk of false positives or negatives that could impact patient care.

Impact & Accessibility

This approach offers a cost-effective, faster alternative to traditional MRI for early Alzheimer's Disease detection. By making advanced diagnostic capabilities accessible through low-field MRI, we're helping to democratize access to quality neurological care.

The open-source nature of Clarity means that healthcare facilities worldwide can implement and adapt the framework to their specific needs, furthering our mission of making Alzheimer's diagnosis more accessible globally.

Future Development

We continue to refine the framework, expanding its capabilities to detect other neurological conditions and working to make it even more robust across different scanner types and imaging protocols.
